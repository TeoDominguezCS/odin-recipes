Mixed Berry Pie

Description

This delicious pie was created for a very special new years party! It was originally going to be a guava pie since my neighbor gifted me an entire bag of guavas on a whim but they didn't ripen in time and were rather crunchy and bitter when sliced. Last second I bought a bag of mixed berries and substituted them into my first blueberry pie recipie! The raspberries and blackberries add a depth and sweetness that cannot be achieved just with blueberries alone!

"This is the BEST pie I've ever eaten" - Rocio

Ingredients

  • Two piecrusts
  • One bag of frozen mixed berrys (900g)
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 Tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 Teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 Tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 Teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 Tablespoon cubed unsalted butter
  • Egg wash: 1 egg beaten with 1 Tablespoon milk
  • A sprinkle of course sugar

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine mixed berrys, sugar, flour, cornstarch, ground cinnamon, lemon juice and zest. Be sure to mix thoroughly until not dry/powdery. You may need to burst a few berrys to moisten.
  2. Preheat oven to 425F
  3. Prepare bottom pie crust onto pie dish
  4. Add filling to pie dish and add cubed butter evenly ontop
  5. Cut designs for pie vents with a sharp knife (I made my vents into stars!)
  6. Cover top of pie and flute crust with a fork
  7. Use extra dough to create more designs (I had enough to make a 2024 and two hearts!)
  8. Assemble designs and coat entire top with a layer of eggwash
  9. Sprinkle top with course sugar as desired, go crazy here to make the crust extra tasty
  10. Bake at 425F for 25 minutes
  11. Add pie shield and reduce temperature to 375F and bake for 50 more minutes
  12. Remove pie and let cool for 4 hours before serving!
